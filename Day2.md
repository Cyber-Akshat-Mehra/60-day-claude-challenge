# Section 1: Explanation

**What is Prompt Engineering?**
Prompt Engineering is the skill of writing clear, specific instructions when talking to an AI tool (like Claude) so it understands exactly what you want and gives you the best possible answer. Think of it like giving directions to a very capable assistant — the clearer your directions, the better the result.

**Why it matters when using AI tools like Claude**
AI models don't "read your mind" — they respond based on the words you give them. A vague prompt leads to a vague, generic answer. A well-structured prompt leads to a focused, useful, ready-to-use answer. Learning this skill means you get more value out of every AI interaction, save time on back-and-forth edits, and unlock capabilities most casual users never discover.

**Three practical benefits of writing better prompts:**
1. **Saves time** — fewer follow-up corrections because you get closer to the right answer on the first try.
2. **Improves output quality** — more relevant, detailed, and usable results (right tone, right format, right depth).
3. **Unlocks advanced use cases** — better prompts let you use AI for real work like coding, strategy, writing, and analysis, not just simple Q&A.

# Section 2: Weak vs Improved Prompt Example

**❌ Weak Prompt:**
"Write about marketing."

*Result: A generic, unfocused paragraph that could apply to anyone, in any industry, for any purpose.*

**✅ Improved Prompt:**
"Act as a marketing strategist. Write a 150-word LinkedIn post explaining 3 low-budget marketing strategies for a small bakery business. Use a friendly, conversational tone and end with a question to encourage comments."

*Result: A specific, ready-to-post piece of content tailored to a clear audience, format, tone, and goal.*

**Why the improved version works better:** It defines the *role* (marketing strategist), the *format* (LinkedIn post, word count), the *audience* (small bakery), the *content* (3 strategies), the *tone* (friendly), and the *goal* (engagement). Every added detail removes ambiguity.
